About Ezra Altabet

In 2019 I graduated with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Maryland in College Park. I completed an Engineering Honors Thesis, developing the first ever prototype of a low-cost upper-extremity rehabilitation robot. After graduating I worked for two years as a device engineer with NextStep Robotics, a rehabilitation robotics startup in Baltimore, MD, where I transformed my proof-of-concept prototype into a clinical trials ready rehabilitation robot. REACH is now a patented device and results of the clinical trials showed clinical efficacy.

In 2021 I went on to graduate study at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, achieving an M.S. in Robotics and a Graduate Certificate in Computational Neuroscience. During my time there I redirected my focus to autonomous robotics. In addition to student collaborations I had the opportunity to work with lead researchers and teams in their field such as the Multi-Agent Autonomy Research Group at General Dynamics Mission Systems, and Necmiye Ozay, a core member of the University of Michigan Robotics Department and affiliate of the Michigan Institute for Data Science.
